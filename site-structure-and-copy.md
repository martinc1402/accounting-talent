# AccountingTalent.in — v1 Site Structure & Copy
### Seeding phase: worker-first, with an employer waitlist

---

## STRATEGIC DECISIONS BAKED INTO THIS STRUCTURE

1. **The homepage speaks to workers, not employers — for now.** Your only goal for the next 3–6 months is filling the database. At employer launch, you flip the homepage to employer-facing (OnlineJobs.ph style) and move worker recruitment to /careers. Build with that flip in mind: keep the hero section a swappable component.
2. **Ads should NOT land on the homepage.** They land on **/apply**, a stripped page (no nav, one CTA) that frames the form. The homepage is for organic, referral, and "who are these guys?" verification traffic — people who saw the ad and Googled you.
3. **Honesty is the brand.** Every Indian accountant has seen scammy "earn dollars from home" pages. Your differentiators are: real salary math, an explicit "employers arrive in [MONTH]" admission, and no fake urgency. This candor is also what makes the referral loop work — nobody refers a friend to something that smells off.
4. **v1 is five pages.** Home, /apply, /for-firms, /faq, /legal (privacy + terms combined). Nothing else. No blog yet — SEO content comes after the funnel converts.

### SECTION 2 — THE MATH (headline: "Why direct hiring changes everything")

Right now, if you do US accounting work in India, you're probably in one of two situations:

**Working at an offshore firm.** The firm bills your US client $12–20 per hour for your work. You see $3–5 of it. The gap pays for their office, their sales team, and their margin — not you.

**Working domestically.** A qualified accountant in a tier-2 city earns ₹25,000–50,000 per month doing work that US firms happily pay 2–4x more for.

**Direct hiring closes the gap.** When a US firm hires you directly, the agency margin doesn't disappear — it gets split between you and them. The firm pays less than an agency charges. You earn more than an agency pays. Everyone wins except the middleman.

### SECTION 6 — SHORT FAQ (top 4, link to /faq for the rest)

**Is this really free?**
Yes, permanently, for accounting professionals. US firms pay a subscription to search the database. If anyone ever asks you for money to join, it's not us.

**I don't have US experience. Should I still apply?**
Yes — if you're a CA, CA Inter, CMA, or an experienced accountant. Many US firms train strong fundamentals. You'll be in the "trainable" pool, shown to firms that hire for potential.

**How would a US firm pay me?**
Directly, in USD, via international transfer or payment platforms — the same way thousands of Indian freelancers and remote workers are paid today. We're also building payment tooling that handles the FIRC paperwork you need for GST/tax filing. More on this in the FAQ.

**Do I have to work US night hours?**
No. Many roles (bookkeeping, tax prep) work fine on Indian hours with overnight turnaround. Roles needing live collaboration pay more for evening overlap. You declare your preference in the application.

# PAGE 4: /faq (WORKER-FOCUSED — the 6 from the homepage plus:)

**What is the verification assessment?**
A short written prompt (describe an accounting problem you solved) plus 10 questions on US accounting and tax basics. It takes ~20 minutes. It exists because "Verified" is what makes US firms trust the database — and what gets you hired faster.

**What if I only know Tally?**
Apply anyway and answer honestly. You'll join the training pipeline — we'll point you to free QuickBooks Online certification (Intuit's is free) and you can upgrade your profile once you complete it. Do not claim software you can't use: firms test in interviews, and false claims get profiles removed permanently.

**Is this legal? Do I need a company or GST registration?**
Working for a foreign client as an Indian freelancer/contractor is legal and common. Depending on your income you may need GST registration (exports of services are zero-rated, but paperwork — FIRC/FIRA — matters). We publish plain-language guides on this before launch, and our payment tooling is being built to generate the right documentation automatically. (We're a platform, not tax advisors — for your personal situation, consult a CA. You probably know one.)

**Can I apply if I'm currently employed at an offshore firm?**
Yes. Your profile is not public to everyone — only paying, verified US firms can browse. You control what identifying detail appears, and you choose when to respond to contact.

**What happens to my data?**
Shown only to subscribed US employers, never sold, deletable on request. Full policy at /legal.

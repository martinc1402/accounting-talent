# Stage 2 Assessment — Complete Package
### AccountingTalent.in verification: writing prompt, quiz, pass logic, emails

**What this is:** the assessment your site promises shortlisted applicants "within 3 days." Passing it earns the Verified badge — the core trust asset of the entire product. Design principles: hard enough that the badge means something, fair enough that a strong candidate without US experience isn't tricked, and built knowing that (a) answers will eventually circulate in WhatsApp groups and (b) some applicants will paste AI-generated writing samples. Both are addressed below.

**Delivery mechanics (v1):** a Tally form, linked from the delivery email with the applicant's ID as a hidden URL parameter. Manual review against this document. No enforced timer in v1 (a stated expectation is enough); 7-day completion window.

---

## PART 1 — The Writing Prompt

Shown as the first section of the assessment form:

> **Tell us about a real accounting problem you solved.**
>
> Describe one specific problem from your own work — a reconciliation that wouldn't balance, books that were behind, a tax return complication, a cleanup project — and how you solved it, step by step.
>
> **Write 80–200 words, in your own words.** Be specific: real numbers, real software, real steps. You don't need perfect English — you need clear English.
>
> **Two things to know before you write:**
> 1. If you pass, this answer appears on your profile, word for word, under "In their own words." US firms will read it when deciding whether to contact you. Write it for them.
> 2. Write it yourself. Generic or AI-written answers are easy to recognize and are rejected regardless of your quiz score — and firms verify these stories in interviews.

**Why it's worded this way:** "appears on your profile, word for word" converts the assessment from a hoop into an audition — effort goes up, fabrication goes down (the story gets probed in interviews and the applicant knows it). The "clear English, not perfect English" line keeps strong tier-2 candidates from freezing. The explicit AI warning is a deterrent that costs nothing and gives you clean grounds for rejection.

### Writing sample rubric (reviewer-facing)

**PASS — needs all four:**
1. **Specific and first-hand:** concrete details that only someone who did the work would include — amounts, software names, time periods, the actual steps. ("The bank feed duplicated three months of transactions and the register was off by $18,400" passes; "I always ensure accuracy in reconciliations" fails.)
2. **Coherent structure:** problem → what they did → outcome. Order can be rough; the logic must be followable.
3. **Comprehensible professional English:** grammar slips are fine; the reader must never have to guess the meaning.
4. **On-length and on-topic:** roughly 80–200 words, about an accounting problem (not a career journey or a motivation statement).

**FAIL — any one of:**
- **Generic/AI-pattern:** no concrete specifics; interchangeable with any other applicant's answer; stock phrases ("in today's fast-paced world," "attention to detail is my passion"); polished but empty.
- **Duplicate:** matches or near-matches another applicant's submission. **Run every sample against previous submissions (exact and fuzzy match) — WhatsApp answer-sharing will happen, and duplicates are your cleanest catch. Flag both parties.**
- **Incomprehensible:** meaning cannot be recovered.
- **Off-topic or far off-length.**

Borderline cases (specific but thin, or one confusing sentence in an otherwise good answer): pass if you would be comfortable showing it to a CPA firm partner with the applicant's name on it. That is the actual bar.

## PART 2 — The Quiz (10 questions, multiple choice)

**Calibration target:** a candidate with real US-client experience scores 8–10. A qualified Indian accountant with zero US exposure scores 4–6 (they get the universal fundamentals, miss the US-specific items). A non-accountant fails badly. Questions 4, 5, and 9 are universal accounting; the rest are US-specific by design.

**Form settings:** shuffle answer options per respondent. State at the top: "Answer from your own knowledge. You'll be asked about these topics in interviews with US firms."

---

**Q1.** Which form does an S corporation file for its annual US federal income tax return?
A) Form 1040  B) Form 1120  C) Form 1120-S  D) Form 1065
**Answer: C.** *Tests basic entity-to-form mapping — the alphabet of US tax prep. Wrong answer = has not touched US returns.*

**Q2.** A US partnership reports each partner's share of income and deductions on:
A) Form W-2  B) Schedule K-1 (Form 1065)  C) Form 1099-NEC  D) Schedule C
**Answer: B.** *K-1s are unavoidable in real 1065/1120-S work. Distractor C catches people who've only heard form names.*

**Q3.** In QuickBooks Online, the Undeposited Funds account is used to:
A) Record owner draws  B) Hold customer payments until they are grouped into a bank deposit  C) Track unpaid vendor bills  D) Record payroll tax liabilities
**Answer: B.** *Pure hands-on QBO knowledge — the single most common cleanup issue in US small-business books. Tally-only candidates have never met this account.*

**Q4.** While reconciling a bank account, you find a check recorded in the books that has not yet cleared the bank. This is:
A) A bank error  B) An outstanding check  C) A deposit in transit  D) An NSF check
**Answer: B.** *Universal fundamental. Everyone with real bookkeeping training passes; filters non-accountants.*

**Q5.** Under accrual accounting, revenue is recognized when:
A) Cash is received  B) The invoice is paid  C) It is earned, regardless of when cash is received  D) The bank statement shows the deposit
**Answer: C.** *Universal fundamental (and US GAAP baseline). Same filtering role as Q4.*

**Q6.** Form 941 is filed to report:
A) Annual corporate income tax  B) Quarterly federal payroll taxes withheld and employer FICA  C) State sales tax  D) Partner distributions
**Answer: B.** *Payroll-adjacent work is common in offshore bookkeeping; 941 is the workhorse form. US-specific.*

**Q7.** Form 1099-NEC is used to report:
A) Wages paid to employees  B) Payments to independent contractors  C) Interest income  D) Dividend income
**Answer: B.** *Deliberately about the form's purpose, not the dollar threshold — the reporting threshold changed under recent US law, so threshold-based questions go stale; purpose doesn't. Tests the employee-vs-contractor distinction every US bookkeeper handles.*

**Q8.** The standard filing deadline for a calendar-year S corporation return (Form 1120-S), without extension, is:
A) April 15  B) March 15  C) June 30  D) January 31
**Answer: B.** *The best discriminator in the set: anyone who has lived a US tax season knows pass-through returns are due March 15; everyone else confidently picks April 15.*

**Q9.** Accounts Payable appears on the:
A) Income statement, as an expense  B) Balance sheet, as a liability  C) Balance sheet, as an asset  D) Cash flow statement only
**Answer: B.** *Universal fundamental. A miss here plus a miss on Q4/Q5 means the applicant is not an accountant.*

**Q10.** In the United States, sales tax is:
A) A federal tax filed with the IRS  B) Levied by states and localities and filed with state authorities  C) Equivalent to GST and filed monthly with the IRS  D) Included in the federal income tax return
**Answer: B.** *Targets the most common India-to-US mental transfer error (GST → "federal sales tax"). Distractor C is bait for exactly that assumption.*

**Answer key:** C, B, B, B, C, B, B, B, B, B. *(Yes — shuffle options per respondent; the raw key's B-heaviness disappears once shuffled.)*

### Answer-leakage plan (be realistic: this quiz will be shared)
- **v1 (now):** option shuffling + the writing sample as the unfakeable gate + duplicate detection. A leaked quiz key gets someone to the writing sample, which they still can't fake well.
- **v2 (when volume justifies, ~200+ assessments):** build two more question variants per slot (same topic, different specifics — e.g., Q8 becomes the 1065 deadline; Q1 becomes "which form does a C corporation file") and rotate randomly. Keep difficulty constant per slot.
- **Always:** the delivery email and form say answers are probed in interviews — makes cheating self-defeating, which is the cheapest deterrent there is.

## PART 3 — Pass Logic

**VERIFIED = quiz ≥ 7/10 AND writing sample = PASS.**

The writing sample is the true gate: a 10/10 quiz with a failed sample is a FAIL (it suggests a leaked key or an AI-assisted run). A 7/10 quiz with an excellent, specific sample is a clean PASS.

**No discretionary passes below 7.** A 6/10 with a great sample is a near-miss, not an exception — exceptions are how badges die. They get the warm fail email and a retake.

**Score display:** show the quiz score on the profile ("Verified 12 Mar 2026 · US tax & accounting exam 8/10"), per the profile spec. 7s sell fine next to a strong writing sample; hiding scores protects nobody.

**Retake policy:** one retake, no sooner than 30 days after the fail, using a different question variant once v2 variants exist (until then, same quiz — acceptable, because the writing sample is re-reviewed and must be a *new* story). Two fails = waitlisted for the training pipeline, not banned.

**Segment expectation-setting (for your dashboard, not public):** fast-track applicants (current US-client experience) should pass at 70–85%. If they're failing in bulk, the quiz is miscalibrated — recheck before doubting the applicants. If Tally-only applicants are *passing* in bulk, you have a leak.

## PART 5 — Review Ops (the 3-day promise, kept)

- **Trigger:** Stage 1 routing (per the form spec) marks FAST-TRACK → send Email A within 24h; STANDARD → within 3 days. Batch once daily; a calendar reminder is sufficient infrastructure at current volume.
- **Review queue:** a sheet/table with: applicant_id, submitted_at, quiz_score (auto from Tally), writing_pass (Y/N/borderline), fail_reason, duplicate_check_done (Y), reviewed_at, email_sent (A/B/C + date). The SLA to protect: **assessment reviewed and result email sent within 3 days of submission** — same number as the delivery promise, one consistent rhythm.
- **Duplicate check:** before any pass, search prior writing samples for matching phrases (exact string search is enough in v1).
- **Calibration log:** record pass rate by Stage 1 segment weekly (fast-track vs standard vs waitlist-upgraded). This is the quiz's health monitor per Part 3.
- **Time cost estimate:** 3–5 minutes per review. Fifty applicants a week ≈ three hours. When it exceeds that, that's the signal to build the v2 variants and semi-automate scoring — a good problem.
